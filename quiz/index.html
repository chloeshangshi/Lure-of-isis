How are most western recruits first getting introduced to ISIS?

While many assume that people become radicalized after coming across extremist content on the Internet, that is rarely — if ever — the case. People are almost always first drawn to extremist ideology is in the real world. Rather than starting someone on the path to radicalization, the Internet adds to an existing path.

Next Question

If people aren’t getting introduced to ISIS online, how are they getting introduced to extremist views?

In the UK and France, universities, prisons, mosques, and the media/news have a history of providing a gateway into radicalization. Universities, prisons and mosques can provide a space for extremists to share their views while the media can expose people to the idea of extremism.

Why has ISIS been so successful recruiting westerners compared to Al Qaeda?

ISIS has benefited from decades of indoctrination from other extremist groups, such as Al Qaeda. While Al-Qaeda said there would be a caliphate one day, they didn’t declare the caliphate, as ISIS has. They’ve also used the Internet to distribute impressive amounts of propaganda.

Next Question

On average, how much online propaganda does ISIS produce daily?

The Quilliam Foundation, a London-based think tank focused on counter extremism, found that, the group produced a daily average of 38.2 unique pieces of propaganda,such as photo essays, videos, audio statements, news bulletins, posters, and theological essays.

About how many Americans have joined or have tried to join ISIS?

More than 250 people living in the United States have joined or attempted to join ISIS, according a Homeland Security Task Force report published in Sept. 2015.

How many investigations into possible extremists does the FBI have open in the US?

As of November 2015, the FBI has nearly 1,000 investigations into possible ISIS supporters open in the United States.
